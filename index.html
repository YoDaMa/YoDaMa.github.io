About

Employing a portion of one’s imagination, YODAMA is a way to shorten my full name Yoseph Daniel Maguire. Any depth of meaning ascribed beyond that would be more akin to creation than analysis.

I am a junior at Rice University studying Electrical and Computer Engineering. I chose to pursue my course of study because it frustrated me that I had no clue how my phone or laptop worked while using them so consistently. I stayed my course because I have yet to study a topic that I found initially intuitive.

I was born in Bangkok, Thailand. At age three I moved to Balikpapan, Indonesia, a small city on the southeast coast of the island of Borneo. By 2nd Grade I moved to Jakarta, Indoensia, and entering 5th Grade I moved to Cairo, Egypt, the city where my mother grew up. At age 13, entering 8th grade, I moved again, this time to Houston, Texas, where I have remained ever since.

In my free time I do research in mobile health technologies, related to activity tracking and analysis. I perform as a player in Rice University's premier (only) Improv Comedy Group, Spontaneous Combustion. I enjoy good coffee, most any art form from the impressionist era, history. I'm introverted, but challenge myself by practicing public speaking, and performing as a player in Rice University's Spontaneous Combustion Improv Group.

I’d like to have an impact in what I do, just so long as that impact is positive.
