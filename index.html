YODAMA stands for Yoseph Daniel Maguire, taking the first two letters of my first middle and last name and combining them. It pretty much has no real meaning beyond it's symbolic personal meaning. I also like how it sounds.

I am a junior at Rice University studying Electrical and Computer Engineering, specializing in Data Science. Electricity has intrigued me since I was young, and I chose to pursue my course of study because it satisfied my long time desire to learn about the entire world of knowledge that allows for such technology as phones and computers to exist. My intellectual journey through the field has been difficult, but also incredible, and the more I learn the more captivated by the knowledge I become.

I was born in Bangkok, Thailand. At age three I moved to Balikpapan, Indonesia, a small city on the southeast coast of the island of Borneo. By 2nd Grade I moved to Jakarta, Indoensia, and entering 5th Grade I moved to Cairo, Egypt, the capital of Egypt and the city where my mother grew up. At age 13, entering 8th grade, I moved again, this time to Houston, Texas, where I have remained ever since.

I enjoy good coffee, most any art form from the impressionist era, history, and probability. My ideal spot is reading, writing, or thinking in a quiet place. I'm introverted, but challenge myself by practicing public speaking, and performing as a player in Rice University's Spontaneous Combustion Improv Group.
