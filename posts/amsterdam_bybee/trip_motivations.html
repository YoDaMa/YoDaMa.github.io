    An overview of the motivations for travelling to Amsterdam

    Published November 30, 2017

    The purpose.

    In a digital age, the impacts of Electrical Engineering are nearly ubiquitous. Very few corners of society have not been radically impacted by the innovation and research in the field. One very interesting intersection is between Digital Signal Processing and Art Conservation. Under the guidance of Dr. Don Johnson, I am travelling to Amsterdam to meet with conservators and researchers using DSP (Digital Signal Processing) techniques to innovate in art related fields. This includes conservation, but expands to all ways DSP is used for the detailed analysis of art.

    A word of thanks.

    This trip is supported by the Rice University ECE Department through the Bybee Travel Award. I am extremely grateful for the opportunities afforded to me throughout my university experience. Rice ECE is a special department and a special community.

    Why Amsterdam?

    Since I took ELEC 241 in the Fall of my sophomore year, Dr. Don Johnson has spoken about his research and work in thread counting for art institution across the globe. His collaborations with the Van Gogh Museum have been an inspiring application of Digital Signal Processing. After all, not many people think of Electrical Engineering when they think of Art Conservation in Amsterdam. Desiring to exploring the intersection of Art and ECE further, I reached out to Dr. Johnson and received funding through the Bybee Travel Award to conduct and independent study in Amsterdam. Dr. Johnson has served as my faculty advisor in planning and connecting with researchers and conservators in Amsterdam.
